Clinician & responder information

MYQER is a patient‑owned emergency card that displays and speaks time‑critical information at the scene — even without signal.

1) Access

Scan the patient’s QR tag / wristband / sticker, or open the phone widget. The card loads instantly; tap Play for a concise voice summary.

2) You’ll see

  • Identifiers (name, DOB), allergies, key conditions, medications.
  • ICE contacts and patient notes.
  • Language aid and the user‑selected triage color.
  • Time‑stamped “Last updated”.

3) Offline ready

The card is cached on the device. If there’s no network, the screen and voice still work. No login is required at scene.

Verification & authenticity

The MYQER card is patient‑supplied information. If risk is high or details conflict, verify with secondary sources (wristband labels, medication boxes, GP/EHR records, Summary Care Record, or next‑of‑kin).

  • Last updated: displayed on the card.
  • Cross‑check: scan tag + confirm name/DOB with patient or ICE contact when possible.

Privacy, consent & security

  • Data is stored on the patient’s device; sharing occurs only when they (or a guardian) present the card.
  • Use for direct care under standard emergency consent exceptions where applicable.
  • Avoid photographing the card unless required by local policy and necessary for care.

MYQER is an information aid, not a medical record. Clinical judgment remains paramount.
