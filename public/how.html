How MYQER Works

MYQER™ is a universal emergency QR card designed to support both individuals and responders.

It doesn't replace clinical records or professional judgment — it simply provides essential information, instantly accessible, to help bridge the gap when seconds matter and details are missing.

⸻

How MYQER Works (Visual Flow)

📱

Step 1 — Scan

A responder scans the MYQER code (on card, sticker, or device).

⬇️
🚦

Step 2 — Triage Pill

A quick status indicator appears to help responders prepare.

🟢 Green 🟠 Amber 🔴 Red
⬇️
📄

Step 3 — Emergency Snapshot (Offline)

If no internet, a short text profile displays directly from the QR:

MYQER — Emergency Snapshot
Name: Jane Doe
DOB: 01 Jan 1990
Blood: O+
Allergies: Penicillin
Conditions: Asthma
Medications: None
ICE: John Doe — +44 7700 123456
⬇️
🌐

Step 4 — Full Profile (Online)

If internet is available, the full, updated profile opens with comprehensive medical information.

⸻
🔄

One QR. Always the same.

  • Every person receives a single QR code that never changes.
  • Print it, carry it, or display it — the QR remains valid for life.
  • Update your profile anytime; your QR will always point to the latest information.
📶

Online or Offline — Always Accessible

When scanned online:

  • Name and Date of Birth
  • Blood Type and Organ Donor status
  • Allergies and Medical Conditions
  • Current Medications
  • Implants & Devices
  • Emergency Contacts (ICE)

When scanned offline:

The QR reveals a snapshot of core details directly, without internet.

🚨

Triage at a Glance

Each card displays a simple triage pill as a visual prompt:

  • 🟢 Green — stable / standby
  • 🟠 Amber — important considerations
  • 🔴 Red — critical attention required

This quick indicator helps responders prepare before reviewing full details.
